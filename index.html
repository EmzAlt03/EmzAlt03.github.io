My Decision To Study At RMIT

Although my prior educational experience, mainly VCE, assisted in obtaining basic knowledge and skills in a variety of fields and a general sense of my limits as an individual, the choice to enroll in a Bachelor of Information Technology was an obvious choice given my acute fascination in the academically diverse course. The choice to indulge in the bachelor's program was based on my goal to enhance my understanding about the field, to seek to secure a place among the variety of available employment opportunities and to further legitimize my educational status. Growing up in the age of technological revolution, my interaction with computing and telecommunications devices was inevitable beginning at the age of 6. My initial interaction revolved basic web surfing, watching YouTube tutorials and Rom/DVD based emulation. As a result of this discovery, I was instantly captivated by IT's complexity, expansiveness and overall implementation in everyday life.

Why I choose RMIT?

Although the concept of completing VCE and moving into adulthood was frightening at first, the prospect of finding employment in the IT industry through a ternary course, invigorated my confidence, self-esteem and hope for the future. Through VTAC, I was presented with a variety of courses in which I studied intuitively for hours. After consulting with professionals, completing quizzes and analyzing my understanding of basic elements I made an informed choice to study a Bachelor of Information Technology. The only difficulty was the variety of ternary education providers across Victoria. I began to explore my options, looking for a provider that follows a form of blended learning. Through a quick array of google searches, I came across the Royal Melbourne Institute of Technology and was certain that this would be my home for the next 3-4 years beginning in 2022.

Expectations at RMIT

The Bachelor’s Degree in Information Technology lasts approximately four years or 16 semesters total. Review of the RMIT homepage highlights a basic course structure with 32 units to be completed, some of which are minor subjects. The degree is built on the credit point system with each course delivering 12 credit points for a passing grade. The subjects I’m most interested in revolving around cyber security, system maintenance and database design. The course covers a variety of fields including programming, cloud computing, user authentication, user centered design etc. The difficulty of understanding concepts varies between subject with some more practical than others. Overall, my personal expectations for the course are of a high standard though, hard work and dedication to my studies including assessments will most definitely resolve many personal doubts along the way
